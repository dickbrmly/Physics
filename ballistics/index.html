Ballistics (with perfect alignment), is only a two demensional event. There is an inital force that converts to a speed which is converted to a height against the constant force of gravity.

Only vertical velocity is affected by gravity. Horzontal velocity continues to the point of impact and maximum vertical velocity is dependent on surface terrain.

Time is relative as they say. I didn't take the time to determine calculation rates on host computers. The rates are non-constants in anycase. So, the cannon ball may not move at the calulated travel time.
